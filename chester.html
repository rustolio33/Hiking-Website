Chester Valley Trail

Stretching from Exton to Upper Merion Township in Montgomery County, the Chester Valley trail provides a place for walkers, runners and cyclists to enjoy the outdoors. It was built on an abandoned railway and is very flat and paved. The trail is paved and is handicap accessible. A number of parks connect to the trail. Plans are to connect the trail to the Schuylkill River Trail.

For more information, visit:
http://www.chesco.org/1239/Chester-Valley-Trail

Marsh Creek State Park

Marsh Creek State Park is home to 535 acre lake with surrounding woodland and trails. The trails are accessible from the West Boat launch and are suitable for hikers, trail runners, horseback riders and mountain bikers. The lake is great for kayaking, paddleboarding, sailing and fishing. If you need a break from the trail, you can rent a canoe, kayak or sailboat during the summer season.

For more information, visit:
http://www.dcnr.state.pa.us/stateparks/findapark/marshcreek/

Schuylkill River Trail (Chester County segment)

Stretching 130 miles from Auburn in Schuylkill County to Philadelphia, the Schuylkill River Trail is a blend of roads and trails that generally follows the path of the Schuylkill River. The Chester County section starts where the trail crosses the Schuylkill River from Montgomery County south of Pottstown and continues through northern Chester County to Phoenixville. It is on roads from Pottstown to Parkerford where it then follows an abandoned railroad to Phoenixville. From there it crosses the Schuylkill River back into Montgomery County

For more information, visit:
http://schuylkillrivertrail.com/

Stroud Preserve

Situated in the rolling hills of Chester County, Stroud Preserve is a mixture of 571 acres of meadow and woodland that is managed by Nature Lands Trust. The preserve is next to the east branch of the Brandywine River. The gravel and off road trails are accessible to hikers, trail runners and horseback riders. Mountain biking is prohibited.

For more information, visit:
https://natlands.org/preserves-to-visit/list-of-preserves/stroud-preserve/

Natural Lands Trust

Natural Lands Trust is an organization dedicating to preserving and managing land. They have 43 preserves totaling 22,000 acres in Eastern Pennsylvania and New Jersey. The land consists of woodland and active farmland that may be owned by private land owners or municipalities, but are dedicated to never be developed. The preserves have many trails and interesting features and are open to the public to enjoy. When visiting a preserve managed by Natural Lands Trust, stay on designated trails and respect private property.

For more information, visit:
https://natlands.org/
